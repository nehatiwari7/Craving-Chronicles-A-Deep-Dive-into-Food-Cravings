🍫 Craving Chronicles: A Deep Dive into Food Cravings

A survey-based statistical study exploring the psychological, physiological, and demographic drivers behind food cravings, using Ordinal Logistic Regression, Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA), and Structural Equation Modeling (SEM).

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Food cravings are shaped by more than just hunger — emotions, habits, and personal circumstances all play a role. This project analyzes survey data from **415 respondents** (age 14–55, Mumbai Suburban region) to statistically identify what actually drives craving intensity, moving beyond assumptions to evidence-based conclusions.

---

## 🛠️ Methodology

- **Survey Data Collection:** 415 respondents, ages 14–55
- **Ordinal Logistic Regression:** to test which demographic factors significantly predict craving intensity
- **Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA):** to reduce a large set of survey variables into a smaller set of meaningful underlying factors
- **Structural Equation Modeling (SEM):** to test and validate the relationships between those factors and craving intensity

---

## 📊 Key Findings

### 1. Demographic Predictors (Ordinal Logistic Regression)
- **Occupation** and **family type** were statistically significant predictors of craving intensity (**p < 0.05**)
- Homemakers showed **5.07× higher odds** of experiencing intense cravings compared to the baseline group

### 2. Underlying Factors (Exploratory Factor Analysis)
20 survey variables were reduced into **4 interpretable factors**, together explaining **62.8% of total variance** (KMO = 0.912, indicating strong sampling adequacy):

| Factor | Description |
|---|---|
| Anticipatory Relief | The comfort/relief anticipated before giving in to a craving |
| Craving Emotions | Emotional states associated with craving onset |
| Triggered Craving | Cravings set off by external cues (sight, smell, stress) |
| Physiological State | Bodily/hunger-related signals |

### 3. Validated Model (Structural Equation Modeling)
The final SEM showed strong model fit:

| Fit Index | Value |
|---|---|
| CFI | 0.939 |
| RMSEA | 0.060 |
| GFI | 0.906 |

**Anticipatory Relief** and **Triggered Craving** were found to be statistically significant drivers of craving intensity (p < 0.05), while Craving Emotions and Physiological State did not show a significant direct effect.

---

## ✅ Conclusion

Food cravings aren't just about physical hunger — this study shows they're driven more by the *anticipation of emotional relief* and *external triggers* than by physiological state alone. Demographic context matters too: occupation and family structure meaningfully shape how intensely people experience cravings. These findings suggest that craving-management strategies should target emotional and environmental triggers rather than focusing purely on physical hunger cues.
